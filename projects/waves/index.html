Solving PDEs on ImagesResolviendo EDPs sobre imágenes

A static image is a two-dimensional array of values — which is also exactly what a PDE solver expects as an initial condition. Taking that literally: load an image, interpret each pixel's intensity (or color channels) as the value of a field $u(x, y, 0)$, and then let the field evolve forward in time according to a chosen equation. The result is an animation that shows how the physics of that equation would deform, spread, or oscillate the image.

Three equations were tried: the wave equation, the heat equation, and the Schrödinger equation. Each produces a qualitatively different kind of motion.

Wave EquationEcuación de onda

The 2D wave equation propagates disturbances at a finite speed $c$:

$$\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2} = c^2 \nabla^2 u$$

Each RGB channel is evolved independently. At the start the three channels share the same initial image, so their wavefronts are spatially coherent and the animation still reads as a recognisable picture in motion — the effect is genuinely wave-like, with peaks and troughs radiating outward from every bright edge. As the simulation runs the channels gradually dephase and fine interference ripples accumulate across the field, eventually dominating the image.

Heat EquationEcuación de calor

The heat (diffusion) equation spreads values toward their spatial average at a rate controlled by the diffusion coefficient $D$:

$$\frac{\partial u}{\partial t} = D\,\nabla^2 u$$

Sharp edges blur out immediately and the image progressively melts into uniform grey — exactly what diffusion demands. There is no oscillation, no wavefront, just an irreversible smoothing that erases structure from fine detail to coarse.

Schrödinger EquationEcuación de Schrödinger

The free-particle Schrödinger equation evolves a complex wavefunction $\psi$:

$$i\hbar\,\frac{\partial \psi}{\partial t} = -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\,\nabla^2\psi$$

The image initialises both the amplitude $|\psi|$ and the phase of the wavefunction, encoding hue as phase and lightness as amplitude. Unlike the heat equation, the Schrödinger equation is norm-preserving — probability is conserved — so the field does not decay but instead redistributes through quantum interference. The probability density $|\psi(x,y,t)|^2$ spreads and diffracts into hologram-like patterns, with colour tracking the evolving phase.
